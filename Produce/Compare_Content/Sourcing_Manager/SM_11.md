# Imported vs Domestic Equipment: A Procurement Decision Framework for Biosafety Equipment

## 适用受众
Sourcing_Manager

## 写作角度说明
在生物安全设备采购中，进口设备和国产设备的选择涉及成本、交货周期、技术差异、服务能力和合规适用性等多维度权衡。本角度帮助采购专家建立系统化的对比分析框架，做出最适合企业实际需求的选择。

## 核心论点 / 洞察
The imported vs domestic decision—beyond unit price—must weigh total cost of ownership, lead time impact on project schedules, local service capability, and regulatory compliance applicability to the specific operational context.

## 参考素材
- Total cost comparison: unit price differential (30-50% for equivalent spec), duty/tariff costs, shipping and logistics
- Lead time impact: domestic delivery (4-8 weeks) vs imported (12-24 weeks), project schedule implications
- Customization flexibility: domestic manufacturers' responsiveness to specification modifications
- Regulatory compliance: CNSA test reports (domestic) vs CE/UL (imported), regional acceptance
- After-sales service: local service engineer availability, spare parts inventory, response time guarantees
- Technical specification equivalence: ISO 14644-1 (international) vs GB 50346 (Chinese) parameter comparability
- Reference installation relevance: matching reference customer profile to buyer's operational context
- Payment terms and financing: letter of credit requirements for imports, domestic payment flexibility
- Risk factors: import/export restrictions, geopolitical supply chain disruption, currency volatility
- Hybrid approach: domestic for standard equipment, imported for specialized high-spec applications
