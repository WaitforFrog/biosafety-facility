Material Selection, Surface Finish, and Drainability as GMP Design Requirements

GMP Annex 1 [GMP Annex 1:2023] establishes explicit material and surface finish requirements for equipment in contact with pharmaceutical products or affecting product quality: all product-contact surfaces must be fabricated from 316L stainless steel (not 304 stainless steel), with surface finish specifications of Ra ≤ 0.8 micrometers (not Ra ≤ 1.6 micrometers). Biosafety-HEPA-supply-exhaust units must incorporate full-weld seam construction (no bolted or riveted joints that create crevices for microbial colonization) and must be designed for complete drainability—no dead legs or low points where liquid can accumulate and support microbial growth. Equipment fabricated from 304 stainless steel or with surface finish exceeding Ra 0.8 micrometers will fail GMP compliance audits and require costly retrofit or replacement. Suppliers offering GMP-compliant designs from initial specification eliminate post-purchase redesign costs and regulatory rejection risk.
