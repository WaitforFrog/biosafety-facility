3. In-Situ HEPA Filter Integrity Scanning and Maintenance Validation

Why Installed Filter Performance Diverges from Rated Efficiency in Sealed Chamber Applications

A new H14 HEPA filter rated at 99.995% efficiency represents laboratory performance under controlled airflow conditions (typically 0.5 m/s face velocity). Installed performance depends on multiple variables: (1) seal integrity between filter frame and housing—a 2 mm gap around the filter perimeter can reduce effective efficiency to 95% or lower; (2) filter media damage during installation—finger contact, tool punctures, or improper handling can create localized bypass paths; (3) dust loading progression—as particulate accumulates, pressure drop increases, potentially causing seal separation if not monitored; (4) vibration-induced frame movement—HVAC system vibration can gradually loosen filter frame fasteners, creating micro-leaks. Regulatory agencies distinguish between "rated efficiency" (manufacturer laboratory data) and "installed efficiency" (on-site performance under actual operating conditions). Facilities that rely solely on filter replacement schedules without in-situ scanning cannot detect seal degradation or installation defects until contamination events occur—at which point regulatory remediation costs and facility downtime become severe.
