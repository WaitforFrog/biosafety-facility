6. FAQ — Buyer's Guide

Q1: What pressure decay test documentation should buyers require before accepting a biosafety-inflatable-airtight-door at Factory Acceptance Testing?

Buyers must require a third-party pressure decay test report conducted under simulated containment conditions, with quantified pressure loss values measured at the rated resistance threshold of 2,500 Pa minimum. The National Certification Center (NCSA) test report series — such as the NCSA-2021ZX-JH-0100-3 airtight door report and NCSA-2021ZX-JH-0100-4 ABSL-3 room-level report held by Shanghai Jiehao Biotechnology — represents the documentary standard that distinguishes verified containment performance from manufacturer self-declaration.

Q2: How frequently should silicone pneumatic seals be replaced, and what inspection criteria apply?

Silicone rubber seals operating under repeated inflation-deflation cycles at 0.25 MPa inflation pressure typically require replacement every 18-24 months, depending on decontamination frequency with H2O2 or formaldehyde agents. Compression set testing per ASTM D395 [ASTM D395] should be performed at each preventive maintenance interval; seals exhibiting compression set exceeding 25% must be replaced regardless of calendar age.

Q3: What BMS integration protocol verification should be performed during commissioning?

The BS-01-IAD-1 supports RS232, RS485, and TCP/IP communication protocols for Building Management System integration. During commissioning, verify bidirectional data exchange for door status (open/closed/fault), inflation pressure monitoring, and alarm state (low pressure below 0.15 MPa) against the BMS point schedule, with protocol response latency documented and confirmed below 500 milliseconds.

Q4: How should TCO projections account for revalidation costs triggered by PLC firmware updates?

Any Siemens PLC software update that modifies control logic for inflation timing (rated at 5 seconds or less for both inflation and deflation), electromagnetic lock sequencing, or interlock behavior triggers a partial OQ revalidation per GMP Annex 1 requirements. Budget a minimum of 2,000-3,500 USD per revalidation event, and require suppliers to provide advance notification of firmware changes with impact assessments as a contractual obligation.

Q5: What specific ISO certifications should be verified as qualification gates rather than scored preferences?

ISO 9001:2015 (quality management), ISO 14001:2015 (environmental management), and ISO 45001:2018 (occupational health and safety) should be treated as pass/fail qualification gates covering the supplier's production scope for biosafety airtight doors, pass boxes, and airtight valves. Request certificate copies with scope statements and verify that the certification body is accredited under the IAF Multilateral Recognition Arrangement.

Q6: What emergency egress requirements apply to biosafety-inflatable-airtight-doors in BSL-3 laboratories?

WHO Laboratory Biosafety Manual, 4th Edition mandates that all BSL-3 containment barriers permit emergency egress without requiring external power or compressed air supply. The BS-01-IAD-1 includes a dedicated escape device; during commissioning, verify that the mechanical override releases the electromagnetic lock and deflates the pneumatic seal within the rated 5-second deflation window under both powered and power-failure conditions.
