3. Cross-Functional System Integration: Pressure Cascade Coordination and HVAC Interface Failures

This section maps the specific interface points where mobile-fogging-disinfectors integration fails and identifies the coordination protocols that prevent costly rework.

Why HVAC-Equipment Integration Failures Dominate Field Commissioning Delays

Mobile-fogging-disinfectors performance depends on stable room pressure maintained by HVAC systems; equipment cannot maintain target disinfection efficacy if room pressure fluctuates beyond ±10 Pa during operation. Project managers who treat HVAC commissioning and equipment commissioning as sequential tasks rather than coordinated phases create pressure instability that invalidates disinfection validation. Typical failure: HVAC contractor balances supply/exhaust airflow without accounting for equipment exhaust flow, resulting in room depressurization during equipment operation and failed disinfection efficacy tests.

Pressure Cascade Logic and Door Cycle Transient Response

ISO 14644-1:2024 [ISO 14644-1:2024] specifies room-to-room pressure differentials of ≥15 Pa per cascade level. When mobile-fogging-disinfectors operates, its exhaust flow (≥16 ml/min per manufacturer specification) must be balanced against HVAC exhaust capacity to maintain cascade pressure. A single door opening in a BSL-3 laboratory can create 30-50 Pa transient pressure swings; if HVAC damper response lag exceeds 8 seconds, room pressure undershoots the cascade minimum, compromising containment. Facilities without pressure monitoring during HVAC commissioning discover this failure only during equipment FAT, requiring damper control reprogramming and 2-3 week delay.
