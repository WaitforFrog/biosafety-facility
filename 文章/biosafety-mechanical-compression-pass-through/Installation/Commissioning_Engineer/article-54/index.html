1. Executive Summary

This guide establishes the installation and commissioning procedure for biosafety-mechanical-compression-pass-through (Model BS-02-MPB-1) with emphasis on pressure decay validation, VHP disinfection system interlock verification, and IQ/OQ documentation requirements for GMP-regulated facilities. Three critical procedures determine commissioning success: (1) on-site pressure decay testing using ASTM E779 methodology to verify frame seal integrity under operational conditions, (2) VHP cycle execution with HVAC interlock verification to prevent explosive vapor concentration gradients, and (3) differential pressure sensor calibration and zero-point adjustment prior to system handover. Installation must follow a strict sequence: mechanical mounting and frame sealing precedes electrical interface work, which precedes control system commissioning. All procedures require documented evidence linked to the validation master plan and manufacturer design specifications.

2. Installation Step 1: Foundation Verification and Anchor Embedment Depth Confirmation Before Door Frame Mounting

This section establishes the prerequisite structural conditions and anchor specifications that determine whether the frame will maintain seal integrity under operational pressure cycling.

Prerequisite: Structural Load Capacity and Anchor Embedment Verification

The installation site must provide a concrete or steel substrate with minimum compressive strength of 25 MPa (concrete) or equivalent steel plate thickness of 8 mm minimum. Verify that the wall opening dimensions match the design specification within ±5 mm tolerance; oversized openings require shim plates or frame extension brackets that must be approved by the manufacturer before installation proceeds. Obtain the anchor embedment depth specification from the manufacturer design documentation — for Model BS-02-MPB-1, M12 expansion anchors require minimum 80 mm embedment depth in concrete with minimum 50 mm edge distance from wall perimeter.

Procedure: Anchor Installation Sequence and Torque Specification

Mark anchor hole locations using the manufacturer-provided drilling template, maintaining ±10 mm positional accuracy. Drill pilot holes to the specified depth using a carbide-tipped masonry bit; measure embedment depth with a depth gauge to confirm ±5 mm accuracy before inserting anchors. Install M12 expansion anchors using a calibrated torque wrench set to 80 Nm ± 5 Nm in a cross-pattern sequence (diagonal pairs, not sequential) to ensure uniform load distribution across the frame perimeter.

Embedment Depth 80 mm minimum Depth gauge measurement
Torque Setting 80 Nm ± 5 Nm Calibrated click-type wrench
Edge Distance 50 mm minimum Measuring tape from wall edge

Acceptance: Frame Verticality and Anchor Preload Confirmation

Measure frame verticality using a digital spirit level at four points (top, bottom, left, right) — maximum deviation ±1 mm per meter of frame height, with total frame deviation not exceeding ±3 mm. Verify anchor preload by attempting to rotate each anchor bolt with a wrench — no rotation should occur when applying hand force (approximately 50 N). Document as-found and as-left torque readings for all anchors; any anchor reading below 70 Nm requires re-torquing and re-verification. Frame installation is complete only when all anchors meet preload specification and verticality tolerance is confirmed in writing.

3. Installation Step 2: Mechanical Seal System Assembly and Gasket Compression Verification

This section addresses the critical failure mode where gasket compression is insufficient due to improper door closure sequence or frame misalignment, resulting in pressure decay rates that exceed acceptance criteria during commissioning.

Prerequisite: Gasket Material Certification and Compression Load Calculation

Verify that silicone rubber gaskets (material specification per manufacturer design documentation) are supplied with manufacturer certification confirming compatibility with hydrogen peroxide vapor (H₂O₂) and formaldehyde disinfection agents. Calculate the required compression load based on the door seal perimeter and target compression ratio — for Model BS-02-MPB-1, the mechanical compression system must apply minimum 150 kPa contact pressure across the full gasket perimeter to achieve the design pressure decay rate of ≤0.1 L/s at 25 Pa [ASTM E779-10]. Confirm that the compression mechanism (mechanical lever or pneumatic actuator) is factory-calibrated and includes a load indicator or pressure gauge to verify compression force during installation.

Procedure: Gasket Installation and Compression Mechanism Calibration

Install gaskets into the frame groove following the manufacturer sequence — typically, perimeter gaskets are installed first, followed by corner gaskets, with no gaps or overlaps at joints. Engage the mechanical compression mechanism (typically a lever-operated cam lock or pneumatic actuator) and apply compression force in a cross-pattern sequence (opposite sides alternately) to ensure uniform gasket compression. Measure gasket compression depth at four points (top, bottom, left, right) using a depth gauge or caliper — compression depth should be 8-12 mm for silicone gaskets, indicating proper contact pressure.

Acceptance: Gasket Compression Load Verification and Visual Inspection

Verify that the compression mechanism holds the specified load without drift over a 5-minute hold period — measure load indicator reading at start and after 5 minutes; acceptable drift is ≤5% of target load. Inspect the gasket perimeter visually for uniform compression marks (slight surface deformation indicating contact) and absence of gaps or voids. Document compression depth measurements and load indicator readings as objective evidence; any measurement outside specification requires gasket replacement and re-compression before proceeding to electrical installation.

4. Installation Step 3: Differential Pressure Sensor Calibration and Zero-Point Adjustment in Installed Configuration

This section prevents the common commissioning error of adjusting transmitter zero without first checking for mounting stress, which masks true sensor drift and produces false pressure readings during operational validation.

Prerequisite: Sensor Mounting Verification and Reference Gauge Certification

Verify that the differential pressure transmitter (typically a 0-100 Pa or 0-250 Pa range sensor for biosafety containment) is mounted on the frame with process connections (high-pressure and low-pressure ports) properly torqued to the manufacturer specification — typically 15-20 Nm for 1/4-inch NPT connections. Check mounting torque on the process connection fittings using a calibrated torque wrench; any loose connection must be re-torqued before calibration begins. Obtain a reference pressure gauge with ±0.05% full-scale accuracy and valid calibration certificate (ISO 17025 accredited, calibration date within 12 months) — this reference gauge will be used as the standard for all transmitter calibration.

Procedure: Zero-Point Calibration and Span Verification in Field Configuration

Power up the transmitter for a minimum 30 minutes to allow thermal stabilization. Vent both the high-pressure and low-pressure ports to atmosphere using a manifold block or calibration adapter — do not disconnect process lines. Record the transmitter output reading (typically 4-20 mA or 0-10 VDC) when both ports are at atmospheric pressure — this is the as-found zero reading. Adjust the zero potentiometer (or software zero trim if the transmitter is networked) until the output reading corresponds to 0.0 Pa differential pressure. Apply a known reference pressure (e.g., 50 Pa using a precision pressure pump or calibration standard) to the high-pressure port while venting the low-pressure port to atmosphere. Record the transmitter output at 50 Pa and calculate the error: Error (%) = [(Measured Reading − 50 Pa) / 50 Pa] × 100. If error exceeds ±1% full-scale (±1 Pa for a 0-100 Pa sensor), adjust the span trim potentiometer and re-verify.

Acceptance: Calibration Certificate Documentation and As-Found/As-Left Data Recording

Document the calibration in a formal certificate per ISO 17025 format, including: (1) transmitter model, serial number, and location; (2) as-found zero reading and as-left zero reading; (3) reference gauge model, serial number, and calibration certificate reference; (4) calibration date and technician signature; (5) next calibration due date (typically 12 months from calibration date). Record environmental conditions during calibration (ambient temperature, barometric pressure) — temperature variation >5°C during calibration requires re-verification after thermal stabilization. Attach the calibration certificate to the IQ documentation package; any transmitter that cannot be calibrated to within ±1% FS must be replaced before system commissioning proceeds.

5. Installation Step 4: VHP Disinfection System Interlock Debugging and HVAC Integration Verification

This section addresses the critical safety failure mode where VHP vapor concentration exceeds the lower explosive limit (LEL) in downstream ducts because the HVAC system continues running during VHP introduction, creating an explosive concentration gradient.

Prerequisite: VHP Cycle Specification and HVAC Interlock Wiring Verification

Obtain the validated VHP cycle specification from the manufacturer — this document must specify: (1) pre-conditioning phase duration and target humidity (typically <30% RH); (2) VHP introduction rate and target concentration (typically 0.3-1.5 mg/L); (3) dwell time at target concentration; (4) aeration phase duration and target residual concentration (<1 ppm H₂O₂). Verify that the HVAC system interlocks are wired correctly: HVAC supply damper must close during VHP introduction phase, exhaust damper must close during VHP introduction, and emergency exhaust must activate if H₂O₂ concentration exceeds 5 ppm. Confirm that the Siemens PLC [Siemens PLC] control system has been programmed with the validated cycle parameters and interlock logic — obtain the PLC program documentation and verify that the cycle parameters match the validated specification.

Procedure: VHP Cycle Execution with Real-Time Sensor Monitoring and Interlock Verification

Execute a test VHP cycle with all sensors active and data logging enabled. Monitor the following parameters in real-time: (1) H₂O₂ concentration (electrochemical or IR sensor, range 0-10 mg/L, accuracy ±5% reading); (2) relative humidity (capacitive sensor, range 0-100% RH); (3) temperature (RTD PT100, range 0-100°C); (4) room pressure differential (differential pressure transmitter, 0-250 Pa range). At the start of VHP introduction phase, verify that HVAC supply damper closes within 10 seconds and exhaust damper closes within 10 seconds — record damper closure times. Verify that room pressure remains negative (typically -10 to -20 Pa) during VHP introduction and aeration phases. If H₂O₂ concentration exceeds 5 ppm at any point, verify that emergency exhaust activates within 30 seconds and that the BMS alarm is triggered.

Acceptance: VHP Cycle Log Documentation and Interlock Response Verification

Document the complete VHP cycle in a cycle log that includes: (1) cycle start and end timestamps; (2) peak H₂O₂ concentration and time to reach peak; (3) dwell time at target concentration; (4) total cycle duration; (5) final residual concentration; (6) HVAC damper closure times; (7) room pressure profile during cycle; (8) any alarm events and response times. Compare the recorded cycle parameters against the validated cycle specification — all parameters must fall within the specification ranges. Simulate a high-concentration alarm (manually trigger the H₂O₂ sensor alarm or inject a test signal) and verify that emergency exhaust activates within 30 seconds and that the BMS alarm is logged. Document interlock response times and alarm activation in the cycle log. The VHP system is approved for operational use only when the cycle log demonstrates that all parameters meet specification and all interlocks respond within acceptance criteria.

7. FAQ — Installation & Commissioning Guide

Q1: What is the immediate post-delivery inspection checklist before installation begins?
Upon delivery, inspect the equipment for visible damage (dents, cracks, corrosion) and verify that the model and serial number match the purchase order. Confirm that all accessories specified in the packing list are present (gasket kits, control panel, documentation). Store the equipment in a clean, dry environment at 15-25°C and 30-60% RH until installation begins.

Q2: What are the civil works prerequisites before mechanical installation can start?
The installation site must provide a concrete or steel substrate with minimum 25 MPa compressive strength (concrete) or 8 mm steel plate thickness. The wall opening must be within ±5 mm of design dimensions, and anchor embedment depth must be verified at 80 mm minimum for M12 expansion anchors with 50 mm edge distance from wall perimeter.

Q3: What differential pressure setpoint is typical for biosafety containment zones?
Biosafety Level 2 and 3 containment typically operates at -10 to -20 Pa (negative pressure relative to adjacent areas) to prevent uncontrolled air leakage outward. The differential pressure transmitter must be calibrated to ±1% full-scale accuracy before commissioning to ensure accurate pressure monitoring.

Q4: How can airtightness be verified in the field without specialized equipment?
A basic smoke test can identify gross leaks: introduce smoke or fog into the sealed chamber and observe for visible smoke movement at seams or joints. However, quantitative pressure decay testing per ASTM E779 [ASTM E779-10] (fill to 250 Pa, isolate, measure decay over 1 minute) is required for regulatory compliance and must be performed by qualified personnel with calibrated instrumentation.

Q5: What are the BMS integration communication parameters for Model BS-02-MPB-1?
The equipment supports RS232, RS485, and TCP/IP communication protocols. Verify the Siemens PLC [Siemens PLC] network configuration: baud rate (typically 9600 or 19200 bps for serial protocols), Modbus RTU address (0-247 range), and parity setting (typically even parity). Obtain the communication protocol specification from the manufacturer before network integration.

Q6: What is the recommended maintenance interval for critical sealing components?
Silicone rubber gaskets should be inspected annually for compression set or surface degradation and replaced every 3-5 years depending on disinfection frequency. Solenoid valves and pressure transmitters should be calibrated annually per ISO 17025 [ISO 17025:2017] standards. Maintain a spare parts inventory including gasket kits, solenoid valve cartridges, and pressure transmitter modules to minimize mean time to repair (MTTR).
